## 1. Business Overview

### About the Business

**Accomplish Points Consulting** is a solo professional consulting practice owned and operated by **Mary Cook Swanson**, based in **Bend, Oregon**. The firm evolved from Mary's prior practice, **Swanson Partners, LLC** ("Your Partner in Service"), which she rebranded to reflect a renewed mission after what she describes as "unprecedented change" across civic society and public organizations.

Accomplish Points is dedicated to **re-imagining and re-building civic participation, partnerships, and private and public service**. Mary brings a rare intersectional combination of urban planning expertise, emotional intelligence, and public/private sector economics to help leaders and organizations accomplish what matters most to them.

### Owner Profile

Mary Cook Swanson has 25+ years of professional experience spanning:

- **Education:** M.A. Urban Planning and B.A. Political Science from UCLA, with additional coursework at UCLA School of Law and Anderson Business School
- **Certifications:** A.I.C.P. (American Institute of Certified Planners), PROSCI Change Management (ADKAR), International Research Board (IRB)
- **Career trajectory:** Private sector land use economics → Senior Planner at Clackamas County (~17 years) → Swanson Partners, LLC → Accomplish Points Consulting
- **Awards:** League of Oregon Cities Good Governance Award for Excellence; IAP2 Cascade Chapter Award of Excellence for "Operation Listen"
- **WBE Status:** Certified Women Business Enterprise, State of Oregon (#5490)
- First-generation college student, U.S. Congressional Intern

### Business Model

Mary operates in two engagement modes:

1. **External Consultant** — Hired to provide expertise with the agility and candor of an outside perspective. Scoped project-based or ongoing relationships.
2. **Internal Staff (Interim / Temp / Part-Time)** — Embedded within an organization as staff, aligned with internal teams to deliver within the organizational framework.

## 3. Services Offered

Mary's services are organized into the following categories. Every engagement is **customized and scoped with each client** — there are no rigid packages.

### 3.1 Management & Leadership Development and Support
Thought partnership for leaders: listening, reflection, advising, strategy design, decision-making frameworks, strategic communications, talking points, and representation at meetings. Includes personality assessments (DISC) and support for adaptive leadership styles. Confidential, dedicated ally relationship.

### 3.2 Coaching — All Levels
Personal and team development, thought partnership, alignment of self with group/team needs, assessment before and during career transitions. Emphasizes genuine interest, customization, humor, and confidentiality.

### 3.3 Meeting Design & Facilitation ("Meetings with Mary")
Mary's signature offering. Award-winning facilitation for meetings ranging from office rooms to outdoor retreats. Services include agenda design, materials preparation, goal setting, discussion moderation, and plan/report generation. Scales from single meetings to multi-day retreats to conference-scale sessions. Branded as delivering "Extra-Ordinary" results due to her planning background.

### 3.4 Project Development & Management
Developing initial ideas into clarified programs with detailed scopes of work, stakeholder involvement, resource identification, timeline/budget tracking, and adaptive project management.

### 3.5 Plan & Policy Making
Creating, updating, and revising organizational and public plans and policies. Technical planning assistance, reports, permit applications, fiscal analysis, tradeoff/alternatives analysis, and coordination of bureaucratic and community processes.

### 3.6 Written Deliverables — "Reports for USE (not the shelf)"
Polished, actionable documentation: plans, policies, reports, summaries, bid packages, strategic communications, talking points, and materials for web/social media. Designed to attract support from donors, voters, philanthropy, and partners — not to sit on a shelf.

### 3.7 Interagency & Public Coordination
Building strategic alliances, drafting intergovernmental agreements, issue analysis, policy alternative development, public speaking, and representation at meetings on the client's behalf.

### 3.8 Communication Strategies & Implementation
Organizational and community relationship strengthening through stakeholder interviews, focus groups, task forces, committees, public workshops, trainings, and retreats. Includes materials production for internal/external communication channels.

### Areas of Specialty
Urban and rural planning; land use and real estate analysis; natural resources, parks and recreation; agriculture; transportation and libraries; engagement and decision making; community and organizational development; scenario backcasting and forecasting; budget implications; governance and management; program and project design and implementation.

## 6. Website Requirements

### 6.1 Business Goals for the Website

1. **Establish online credibility** — Mary currently has no active website (Swanson Partners site is legacy). Accomplish Points Consulting needs a professional web presence to validate the business for prospects who search online.
2. **Clearly communicate the service offering** — Prospects should quickly understand what Mary does, who she serves, and how she's different.
3. **Generate inquiries** — The website should drive prospective clients to make contact (phone, email, or contact form).
4. **Showcase expertise and track record** — Credentials, awards, client list, and testimonials must be prominently featured to build trust.
5. **Support the rebrand** — Transition visibility from "Swanson Partners" to "Accomplish Points Consulting" as the current identity.
6. **Serve as a reference point** — When Mary is introduced or referred, the website should serve as a comprehensive profile that reinforces confidence.

### 6.2 Recommended Site Structure

#### Home / Landing Page
- Hero section with Mary's primary professional headshot, business name, and the "Your Access Point to..." value proposition
- Brief overview of who Accomplish Points serves (public organizations, leaders, communities)
- High-level service summary (3–4 key offerings with links to details)
- 1–2 featured testimonials
- Clear call-to-action: "Let's Talk" / contact prompt

#### About / Bio Page
- Mary's professional narrative covering her career arc: UCLA education → private sector → Clackamas County (17 years) → Swanson Partners → Accomplish Points
- Key credentials section: AICP, PROSCI, UCLA degrees, WBE certification
- Awards section
- Professional headshot(s)
- The "intersectionality" message: People + Planning + Economics
- Historical context: the evolution from Swanson Partners to Accomplish Points
- Optional: conference speaking/motivational speaking mention

#### Services Page(s)
This is the most content-rich section. Can be structured as a single long page with anchor links or as a hub page with sub-pages. The services to present are:

1. **Management & Leadership Development** — Thought partnership, strategy, trusted ally
2. **Coaching — All Levels** — Personal/team development, DISC, career transitions
3. **Meeting Design & Facilitation ("Meetings with Mary")** — Award-winning, all scales, retreats to conferences
4. **Project Development & Management** — Scoping, stakeholder engagement, tracking
5. **Plan & Policy Making** — Technical planning, reports, fiscal analysis
6. **Written Deliverables** — "Reports for USE (not the shelf)" — plans, policies, strategic comms
7. **Interagency & Public Coordination** — Alliances, agreements, public representation
8. **Communication Strategies** — Stakeholder engagement, workshops, trainings

Each service should include:
- Clear description of what's included
- Who it's for / when it applies
- What makes Mary's approach distinctive

The **two engagement models** (External Consultant vs. Internal Staff) should be explained, possibly as a separate "How We Work" section or integrated into the services overview.

#### Clients Page
- Client categories: Government, University/OSU, Special Districts, Cities
- Named client list (logos if obtainable)
- Brief case descriptions if Mary provides them later

#### Testimonials Page (or integrated throughout)
- The 4 existing testimonials with attribution
- Space to add more later (Mary has additional unpublished testimonials)
- Consider placing the strongest quotes on the Home and Services pages as well

#### Contact Page
- Contact form (name, organization, email, phone, brief message/interest area)
- Phone number and email (to be provided by Mary — verify current contact info, the legacy info is Swanson Partners era)
- Location: Bend, Oregon (no physical office address needed unless Mary wants one)
- Optional: scheduling link integration if Mary uses a scheduling tool

### 6.4 Feature Requirements

#### Must Have
- **Responsive design** — Mobile-friendly; government and public sector professionals access sites from various devices
- **Contact form** — Simple inquiry form as the primary conversion mechanism
- **Professional, clean design** — Conveys gravitas and expertise without being corporate or cold
- **Fast load times** — Simple site, no heavy frameworks needed
- **SEO fundamentals** — Meta titles, descriptions, and structured content so Mary can be found for consulting terms in Oregon
- **Accessibility (WCAG compliance)** — Public sector clients will expect/appreciate this; it also aligns with Mary's public service values
- **SSL certificate** — Standard security, builds trust

#### Should Have
- **Testimonial rotation or featured quotes** — On home page and/or services pages
- **Downloadable one-pager or capability statement** — PDF version of Mary's firm profile for prospects to share internally (common in government procurement)
- **Blog or insights section** — Mary has been asked to "write a book" by clients; a blog is a lower-commitment way to share thought leadership. Can start empty with infrastructure in place.
- **Analytics** — Basic traffic monitoring to understand who's visiting and from where

#### Nice to Have
- **Scheduling integration** — Calendly or similar for booking introductory conversations
- **Client logo grid** — Visual credibility display if logos are obtainable
- **Newsletter signup** — Email list capture for future content distribution
- **Video or audio introduction** — The PPTX contained multiple audio files (Mary narrating her bio); this content could translate to a brief intro video or audio greeting
- **Case studies section** — Expandable later as Mary documents project outcomes

### 6.5 Technical Considerations

- **Content management:** Mary should be able to update testimonials, add blog posts, and make minor text edits without developer assistance. A simple CMS or static site with a headless CMS would be appropriate.
- **Domain:** Verify whether accomplishpoints.com (or similar) is registered. The site needs its own domain — not a continuation of swansonpartners.com.
- **Hosting:** Lightweight site; static hosting or simple managed hosting is sufficient.
- **Email:** Professional email (e.g., mary@accomplishpoints.com) should be set up alongside the website.
- **Maintenance:** The site should be low-maintenance. Mary is a solo practitioner and will not have IT support.

### 6.6 Design Direction

- **Color palette and typography** should convey the dual tone of "gravitas + friendly" — think earth tones, deep blues or greens with warm accents, professional serif or clean sans-serif fonts
- **Photography-forward:** Mary has strong professional headshots (once unwatermarked versions are obtained); these should feature prominently
- **White space and readability** — Long service descriptions need breathing room; avoid dense blocks of text
- **No stock imagery of generic business people** — Mary's brand is personal and authentic; the site should use her own photos
- **Oregon/Pacific Northwest feel** — Mary's clients are Oregon-based; subtle regional warmth is appropriate
