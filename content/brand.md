## Business Name

## Brand Voice & Tone

Mary explicitly states the tone she wants (slide 2):

> "I need **Gravitas** and **Reliable Expert** and **Friendly** — safe to work on those darn people parts of work and self"

**Translation for web copy:**
- **Authoritative but approachable** — she has deep credentials (UCLA, AICP, 25+ years) but leads with warmth
- **Service-oriented** — language of partnership, support, "dedicated ally"
- **Joyful** — "Humor and Joy of Service" appears in multiple slides
- **Confidential & trustworthy** — "Confidentiality" listed as a core value
- **Customized** — "Genuine Interest in You and customization For You"

## Brand Values

Distilled from across all slides:

1. **Partnership** — Collaborative relationship, not top-down consulting
2. **Customization** — Every engagement scoped for and with the client
3. **Emotional Intelligence** — People-first approach to organizational work
4. **Practical Results** — Deliverables that get used, not shelved
5. **Confidentiality** — Safe space for leaders to grow
6. **Joy in Service** — Enthusiasm and humor as part of the work
7. **Public Service Ethic** — Deep commitment to civic participation and public organizations

From slides 2, 21, and the firm profile documents:

- **Primary:** Public service organizations — cities, counties, special districts, universities

- **Individual:** Leaders and managers at all levels seeking coaching and development
- **Relationship style:** Engagements range from consultant (external, contracted) to interim/temp internal staff roles

## Competitive Differentiators

What makes Mary different from other consultants, in her own words:

1. **Intersectionality of skills:** Urban Planning (technical) + Emotional Intelligence (people) + Economics (practical) — a rare combination
2. **Both internal and external service models:** Can serve as consultant OR embed within organizations as interim staff
3. **Award-winning facilitation:** County and City awards for public engagement processes
4. **Nationally certified planner (AICP)** with a Master's from UCLA — brings technical rigor to people-focused work
5. **Written deliverables:** Doesn't just facilitate — produces polished reports, plans, and strategic communications
6. **Public and private sector fluency:** Bridges both worlds, understands partnerships
