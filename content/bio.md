## Full Narrative Biography

### Early Life & Leadership

Called to public service and bringing people together to get things done, Mary was elected to her first of many student leadership positions in 7th grade. This position launched her into community projects — including park and garden development — as well as teamwork and facilitation. She expanded the scope of the club.

At her school reunion she was asked, "What did your dad do?" — "He sold so many girl scout cookies and stuff at his company for you to win!" In reality, that was all Mary — the girl scout and student selling door to door, winning many sales awards with her enthusiasm and vision, while her dad worked blue-collar shifts to support his family and their futures.

By the time she was in high school she was not only elected to positions within her schools but also was a Junior Statesman and Girls State participant. She designed and attracted participants for a "Government Day in Los Angeles" where students around the region met with public officials and personally observed the actions of area institutions.

### Education

A first-generation college student, Mary worked her way through college attaining a B.A. in Political Science from UCLA. During those years, selected as a U.S. Congressional Intern, she bought herself a ticket to D.C. for her first-ever plane ride and tracked and reported on Congressional policy development.

Always fascinated by the earth, she minored in geography, laying a foundation for later work with land use planning, parks, scientists, and policy makers.

While continuing her work as a consultant, she focused on her Master's Degree in Urban Planning at UCLA. Knowing the value of specific expertise, she independently took courses from the UCLA School of Law and Anderson Business School and excelled in the multi-disciplinary courses that included design and architecture, geography and land use, economics, sociology, governance, and citizenship.

### Early Career — Private Sector

Her chance "to work in the private sector solving public sector problems." She volunteered in relevant work and was thrilled to be offered her dream job, with a mutual two-year commitment to expertise development. There she honed her understanding and skills in land use economics, conducting studies, research, and analytics — all to discover that she was most interested in the meetings: the development and conduct of public and private partnerships and the roles and responsibilities of those involved and affected directly and indirectly.

Mary spent her early career in the private sector performing land use economic and market analyses for public sector agencies.

### County Government — Senior Planner

Her next professional path was public sector in California Redevelopment, followed by independent consulting in Oregon to attain her Senior Planner role in Clackamas County Government.

In her 17 years with the County, she developed a deep understanding of public agencies and strategic and developmental planning processes. Mary's strategic planning and facilitation skills were in high demand and she was used on complex, high-profile special projects for:
- The County Commission
- The Planning Division
- The Transportation Engineering Division
- The Development Agency (Economics)
- The Parks Department
- North Clackamas Parks and Recreation District

The diversity of County work and leaders was foundational to her satisfaction in the "special projects" role she would become known for — facilitating complex people and projects to evolve productively.

**Award-winning projects** for County and City. Change Management Certified. A.I.C.P. National Certification.

Mary performed award-winning work in the design and implementation of public involvement strategies and events for public agencies while at the County and as Principal of Swanson Partners, LLC.

### Swanson Partners, LLC — Independent Consultancy

With curiosity for other public organizations, Mary began consulting again. With prior experience in small consultancy operations, she successfully ran Swanson Partners, LLC and enjoyed her tagline role as "Your Partner in Service."

A trusted ally with unwavering focus on client service, she thrived in the creative role of helping people become better participants in their teams at work, teams improving their agility and clarity of purpose, and organizations becoming more agile and fun.

Organizational Development and Community Development are Personal. Change management is motivational stewardship to accomplishing what is most important to the clients — an organization of people serving a purpose with enthusiasm and enlightenment that their performance matters. A transformational process designed and conducted with expertise to facilitate people's ability to accomplish what is most important to them.

**Recent Swanson Partners clients included:** Oregon Wine Research Institute, Food Innovation Center, Oregon State University's North Willamette Research and Extension Center, Clackamas County Extension and 4-H District, Clackamas County Library District, Tillamook County Transportation District, City of Molalla, and North Clackamas Parks and Recreation District.

Ms. Swanson is also a motivational and instructive speaker at conferences and events regarding "Getting The Most out of Your Planning Process" and topics related to developmental planning and leadership for enhanced personal, organizational, and community achievement.

### Accomplish Points Consulting — Current

After recent years of "unprecedented change" across civic society and public organizations specifically, Accomplish Points is dedicated to re-imagining and re-building civic participation, partnerships, and private and public service.

Mary's expertise encompasses consensus building, process design and facilitation, project scoping and management, and delivery of entrepreneurial energy for clients.

Mary serves in two modes:
1. **As consultant** — with the helpfulness of fresh perspective and agility to focus on performing agreed-upon roles and responsibilities of contracted services. This role allows agility and support for people's growth and teamwork, and often a client candor that facilitates evolution and change.
2. **As internal staff** (interim, temp, or part-time) — with dutiful and enthusiastic alignment with staff and leaders to deliver excellence within an organizational framework when that internal operations role is valuable.

After her clients asked her to "write a book" so she could teach them "how you do what you do," she relocated to sunny Bend, Oregon and remains focused on client services with rededication to the personal skills of consensus building and engagement.

Expert in organizational development, leadership support, emotional intelligence, personal and organizational change, education about citizenship, and necessary skills for re-building the "sense" and ethic of public service.
